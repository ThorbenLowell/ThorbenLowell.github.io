Cygnus (The Swan)

Star Names and Pattern: Cygnus forms a cross-shaped pattern, known as the Northern Cross. The main stars include Deneb ( Cygni), Albireo ( Cygni), Sadr ( Cygni), Gienah ( Cygni), and Rukh ( Cygni).

Magnitude of Alpha and Beta Star:

  • Cygni (Deneb): Magnitude 1.25
  • Cygni (Albireo): Magnitude 3.08

Temperature of Alpha and Beta Star:

  • Deneb: ~8,500 K
  • Albireo: ~4,270 K

Right Ascension & Declination:

  • Deneb: RA 20h 41m 25s | Dec +45 16 49
  • Albireo: RA 19h 30m 43s | Dec +27 57 34

Mythological Figure: Cygnus represents a swan. In Greek mythology, its associated with several myths, including the story of Zeus disguising himself as a swan.

Seasons When Visible: Best visible in the Northern Hemisphere from June to October.

Light Year Distance from Earth:

  • Deneb: ~2,615 light-years
  • Albireo: ~430 light-years

Delphinus (The Dolphin)

Star Names and Pattern: Delphinus forms a small diamond or kite-shaped pattern. The main stars include Sualocin ( Delphini) and Rotanev ( Delphini).

Magnitude of Alpha and Beta Star:

  • Delphini (Sualocin): Magnitude 3.77
  • Delphini (Rotanev): Magnitude 3.63

Temperature of Alpha and Beta Star:

  • Sualocin: ~7,540 K
  • Rotanev: ~7,700 K

Right Ascension & Declination:

  • Sualocin: RA 20h 39m 38s | Dec +15 54 43
  • Rotanev: RA 20h 37m 33s | Dec +14 35 42

Mythological Figure: Delphinus is associated with the dolphin that saved the life of the Greek poet Arion.

Seasons When Visible: Best visible in the Northern Hemisphere from July to October.

Light Year Distance from Earth:

  • Sualocin: ~241 light-years
  • Rotanev: ~97 light-years

Ursa Minor (The Little Bear)

Star Names and Pattern: Ursa Minor is famous for containing Polaris ( Ursae Minoris), the current North Star. The constellation forms a small dipper-like pattern.

Magnitude of Alpha and Beta Star:

  • Ursae Minoris (Polaris): Magnitude 1.97
  • Ursae Minoris (Kochab): Magnitude 2.08

Temperature of Alpha and Beta Star:

  • Polaris: ~6,000 K
  • Kochab: ~4,500 K

Right Ascension & Declination:

  • Polaris: RA 02h 31m 49s | Dec +89 15 51
  • Kochab: RA 14h 50m 42s | Dec +74 09 19

Mythological Figure: Ursa Minor is associated with the myth of Arcas, the son of Callisto, who was transformed into a bear and placed in the sky.

Seasons When Visible: Visible year-round in the Northern Hemisphere due to its proximity to the North Pole.

Light Year Distance from Earth:

  • Polaris: ~323 light-years
  • Kochab: ~130 light-years

Lyra (The Lyre)

Star Names and Pattern: Lyra is a small constellation known for its brightest star, Vega ( Lyrae). The constellation forms a small parallelogram.

Magnitude of Alpha and Beta Star:

  • Lyrae (Vega): Magnitude 0.03
  • Lyrae (Sheliak): Magnitude 3.52

Temperature of Alpha and Beta Star:

  • Vega: ~9,600 K
  • Sheliak: ~13,000 K

Right Ascension & Declination:

  • Vega: RA 18h 36m 56s | Dec +38 47 01
  • Sheliak: RA 18h 50m 04s | Dec +33 21 46

Mythological Figure: Lyra represents the lyre of Orpheus, the musician who could charm anything with his music.

Seasons When Visible: Best visible in the Northern Hemisphere from June to October.

Light Year Distance from Earth:

  • Vega: ~25 light-years
  • Sheliak: ~960 light-years
